About The Device

A Brief History and current position of S20 FE.

The Samsung Galaxy S20 FE was released in October 2020 as a more affordable option than its pricier flagship counterparts.

Samsung knew that people wanted a taste of that premium smartphone experience without having to pay top dollar. So, they crafted the S20 FE to be a crowd-pleaser, offering a range of vibrant colours like Cloud Red, Cloud Orange, Cloud Lavender, Cloud Mint, Cloud Navy, and Cloud White to suit every personality.



But the S20 FE isn't just about looks; it has substance too. It packs a gorgeous 6.5-inch Super AMOLED display with a super-smooth 120Hz refresh rate, making scrolling and gaming a breeze. And let's not forget about the camera system, featuring a versatile triple-lens setup with a 12MP wide lens, an 8MP ultrawide lens, and a 12MP telephoto lens, ensuring you capture stunning photos and videos in any lighting condition.



Under the hood, the S20 FE was powered by the Qualcomm Snapdragon 865 5G processor, one of the best at the time, providing ample power for all your daily tasks and multitasking needs. Plus, its long-lasting 4,500mAh battery kept you going from morning to night, and when it did need a boost, you could quickly top it up with 25W wired fast charging or 15W Qi wireless charging.



The Samsung Galaxy S20 FE was a game-changer, offering a perfect balance of affordability and premium features. It was Samsung's way of saying, "Hey, you don't have to spend a fortune to get a great smartphone experience." I'm not sure about all the people, but most of the consumers seem to have loved it too.

Real Life-Use

Real Life-Use

Simple yet Secure

My experience with the Samsung Galaxy S20 FE's One UI has been nothing short of delightful. It's a perfect blend of functionality and aesthetics, making it a joy to use every day. The intuitive design and thoughtful organization make it easy to navigate through the interface, while the clean and visually appealing design adds a touch of sophistication and makes it even more enjoyable to use. One of the things I appreciate most about the One UI is its focus on one-handed usability. Even on the Galaxy S20 FE's large display, essential elements are easily reachable with one hand. This makes it so much more convenient to use my phone on the go.

The extensive customization options within the One UI are another big plus. I love being able to personalize my experience to suit my individual preferences. From adjusting the theme and icon styles to configuring the always-on display and quick settings, the One UI gives me a sense of ownership over my device. Overall, the One UI on the Galaxy S20 FE is an excellent choice for anyone who appreciates a balance between customization and ease of use. It seamlessly combines a visually pleasing interface with practical functionality, catering to users who seek both personalization and a user-friendly experience. The choice of a user interface is a personal one, and what works for one person may not work for another. Ultimately, the best way to find the right UI for you is to experiment with different options and see what you like best.



Tough And Trust-worthy

I've found that its water-resistant technology, scratch-resistant hybrid plastic back, and wireless charging capabilities blend seamlessly to offer me an experience that feels both luxurious and adaptable. For someone like me who values durability without sacrificing style and functionality, the Galaxy S20 stands out as the preferred choice. It's not just a phone; it's a reliable companion that effortlessly complements my lifestyle.

Samsung Galaxy S20 FE has been my reliable companion through thick and thin. Its sleek design has not only impressed me but has proven its mettle in real-life scenarios. The water resistance feature has been a game-changer on several occasions when I found myself caught in the rain. Despite being exposed to rain multiple times, the phone has never let me down. The flawless condition after an underwater incident speaks volumes about the durability and water resistance engineered into this device.

Moreover, the wireless and reverse wireless charging capabilities of the Galaxy S20 FE have been genuine lifesavers. The convenience of wireless charging has come to the rescue on numerous occasions when I found myself with a low battery and no access to a charging cable. Additionally, the reverse wireless charging feature has allowed me to help out friends in need.


Plenty-Powerful

With is processor as well as 8 GB of RAM, the Galaxy S20 Fe handeled everything that I threw at it. No Lags, no Bottlenecks From Games to Photoediting sessions everything was swift and smooth. I've noticed that my Galaxy S20 FE can sometimes experience minor hiccups during long gaming sessions. These hiccups are rare and don't happen all the time, but they can sometimes cause stuttering or frame drops. This is especially noticeable in graphics-intensive games that really push the phone's hardware to the limit. It's worth mentioning that how often these hiccups happen can vary depending on a few things, like the specific game I'm playing, how hot the phone is, and what other apps are running in the background.

While the Snapdragon 865 processor is a powerful one, it's not perfect, and it can sometimes struggle to keep up with really demanding tasks. Even though these hiccups happen sometimes, I'm still happy with how my Galaxy S20 FE handles demanding tasks overall. It's a great balance of performance and price, and it's been a great choice for me as a gamer who also likes to multitask on their phone. It's important to remember that technology is always changing, and updates to both hardware and software can affect how well a device performs over time. So it's always a good idea to keep up with the latest software updates and read user reviews to make sure your phone is running at its best.

Verdict

To whom is it..??

Would be ideal

  • Budget-conscious consumers: The S20 FE gives you premium features without the premium price.
  • Photography enthusiasts: The camera system takes stunning pictures in all sorts of lighting conditions.
  • Gamers and multimedia users: The S20 FE has a fast processor, lots of RAM, and a beautiful display, making it great for gaming and watching videos.
  • Samsung ecosystem fans: The S20 FE integrates seamlessly with other Samsung devices and services.
  • People who prioritize design: The S20 FE has a sleek and stylish design.

To those who are not so much concerned with the appearance of the device and focused on the functionality of it. It dosent get any better than this. For under 300 CAD this is the best Bang for your buck.


Might not be a great choice

  • Power users: If you need the absolute latest and greatest technology, the S20 FE might not be powerful enough for you.
  • Users who need a lot of storage: The S20 FE only has 128GB of storage.
  • People who want the latest software updates right away: The S20 FE doesn't always get software updates as quickly as flagship models.

For those who proritize the look and feel of their device, I'd say there are better alternatives which will also run you a bit much.
